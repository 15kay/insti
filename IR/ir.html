Analytics & Business Intelligence

The Analytics & Business Intelligence function plays a crucial role in supporting the university's evidence-based approach to planning and decision-making. It relies on data and analysis, facilitated by a robust Business Intelligence solution. This unit conducts strategic evaluations of institutional performance, including benchmarking, performance analysis, annual and midterm reports, and departmental performance profiling.

The function also models future performance trends such as student enrolment projections and carries out impact assessments. It provides the evidence base for programme reviews, strategic initiatives, and ad hoc requests.

Roles and Responsibilities:

  • Data Warehousing: Design, build, and maintain relational databases and data systems for effective storage and analysis. Establish a scalable and secure data warehousing infrastructure.
  • Feeder Schools: Analyse data from feeder schools to gain insights into prospective student populations and demographic patterns.
  • Special Projects: Execute data-driven special projects that align with strategic university goals, providing tailored reporting and analysis.
  • Institutional Performance Monitoring: Track institutional performance metrics, identify gaps, and generate reports to inform continuous improvement across faculties and departments.
  • Institutional Reports and Publications: Develop and share detailed institutional reports with stakeholders to guide policy, planning, and public communication.
  • Self-Service Reporting and Dashboards: Provide tools such as PowerHEDA for users to access real-time data, generate custom reports, and make informed decisions independently.
  • Data Warehouse for Student Success: Manage the integration of Learning Management System (LMS) and other data into analytics platforms that support learning analytics and student success initiatives.
